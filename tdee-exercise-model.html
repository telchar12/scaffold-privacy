TDEE & Exercise Model

Scaffold · How daily calorie and exercise targets are linked

Overview

Scaffold uses one continuous energy model:

  1. BMR from your body metrics (Mifflin–St Jeor).
  2. Exercise target E (kcal/day of structured activity) as the primary activity input.
  3. PAL and TDEE are derived — not edited separately.
  4. Activity level on Profile is a preset that sets a default E.
  5. Small changes in E produce small changes in intake (no activity-tier cliffs).

Core formulas

Basal metabolic rate

BMR = Mifflin–St Jeor(age, sex, weightKg, heightCm)

Physical activity level (continuous)

PAL = 1.2 + E / BMR
  • 1.2 = sedentary baseline (sleep, desk, casual living — no structured exercise).
  • E = daily exercise energy expenditure target (kcal/day).
PAL ≥ 1.2
PAL ≤ 2.5   (long-term sustained cap)

Maintenance TDEE

TDEE = BMR × PAL
     = 1.2 × BMR + E

Both forms match when PAL = 1.2 + E/BMR.

Calorie intake target

Weight-goal adjustment G (kcal/day) is applied after maintenance:

intakeTarget = TDEE + G

Activity presets

Activity level is a shortcut for default E, not a separate PAL source.

E_preset = BMR × (PAL_preset − 1.2)

Sedentary: E = 0 — structured exercise is zero; PAL is exactly 1.2.

The UI may show rounded kcal (for example “~1145 kcal”), but stored and computed values use full precision.

When you pick an activity on Profile and have not customized exercise:

E ← E_preset
PAL ← 1.2 + E/BMR
TDEE ← BMR × PAL

That matches classic BMR × PAL calculators for the same preset.

Weight-goal deficit or surplus is applied through G only — not by nudging the exercise target.

Sustained ceiling

For saved long-term intake targets:

TDEE ≤ 2.5 × BMR
E_max_sustained = 1.3 × BMR
E_clamped = min(E, 1.3 × BMR)
PAL = min(1.2 + E_clamped/BMR, 2.5)

Custom exercise target

StateIntake driver
Auto from activityPreset E; PAL matches the preset
Custom training targetYour E; PAL = 1.2 + E/BMR

Editing the exercise field turns on custom mode and live-recalculates TDEE and intake (subject to the sustained cap).

Reset to activity default restores the preset E.

Because PAL and TDEE are linear in E:

ΔTDEE / ΔE = 1 kcal
ΔPAL / ΔE = 1 / BMR

Example at BMR = 1636:

Changing activity while exercise is customized does not silently overwrite E. Scaffold asks whether to adopt the new activity preset or keep your custom target.

FAQ

Does 700 kcal always mean athlete (PAL 1.9)?
No. Athlete means E = 0.7 × BMR. At BMR 1636 that is about 1145 kcal, not 700. Seven hundred is only 1.9-equivalent when BMR = 1000.

Is this standard TDEE?
It is the additive sedentary-base + exercise form, equivalent to PAL when E = BMR × (PAL − 1.2). Consumer calculators use bundled PAL; sports nutrition often splits base + exercise. Both match when calibrated.

Will 700 → 699 feel smooth?
Yes. One continuous function; intake changes by 1 kcal per 1 kcal of exercise (before goal clamps).

Why cap at 2.5 × BMR?
Long-term sustainability (constrained energy model). Short peaks above that are not part of the default model.
